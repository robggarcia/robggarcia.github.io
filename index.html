About Me

Hello World! My name is Rob Garcia and I love to code! I am an Engineer, Educator, Musician, and Life-long Learner. I am completing courses through Codecademy, freeCodeCamp, and am pursuing an Undergraduate Certificate in Cloud Computing at the University of Virginia.

After teaching Phyiscs and Math in Philadelphia, my wife and I decided to move to Virginia in the summer of 2020 in order to be closer to family. I found a job as Engineering Teacher at Charlottesville High School where I teach students from 9th through 12th grade how to program microcrontrollers and use the OnShape CAD application software in order to 3D print and laser cut their designs. Within this past year, I have completely fallen in love with coding and hope to see where it can take me.

In my free time I like to write and record music, build guitar amps from scratch, learn about signal processing, and enjoy the great outdoors. I've been learning and teaching others to code for the past 3 years or exactly milliseconds!

Projects (click titles to explore)

Arduino Drag Racer

This project is the final project of the year in my Engineering 2 class. It challenges students to code a specific startup sequence that uses a photointerrupter and button to tigger the motors to run. Another key component of the design is to use gears in order to incresae the running speed.

Mini Useless Box

The first assignments for Engineering students at Charlottesville High School is to build a Useless Box. I decided to challenge myself by designing the smallest possible box that still contains all of the necessary components. This site documents my design process and lessons learned.

CHS Engineering Assignments

This is the full collection of Arduino programming assignments that have been developed for Charlottesville High School. Each assignment builds in complexity and provides students with the skills needed to complete future projects.

Playlist App w/ React

This was the first app I've built using React. Learning how to correclty use props and items were a huge hurdle, but I know understand how powerful this Javascript library can be. This app accesses the Spotify API to create and save a playlist to the user's profile.

Pseudo-Science Home

Pseudo-Pscience was crated to be home base for all of the projects I am interested in and currently working working on. When I'm not working or learning how to code, I am either playing music or building electronics. In the future I hope to explore signal processing.
